ABOUT US

The urbanriderz.in story: I joined the IT Firm in 2007 and served for over 8 years. Over those years, I moved many times and put a lot of miles on motorcycles exploring different parts of the places and finding out first-hand the diversity of our country’s roads and scenery. Yet, any of you who have spent a lot of time on a motorcycle know that “not all roads are created equal” and there is nothing like a great motorcycle road. Unfortunately for me, every time I moved, I was faced with the sometimes challenging task of finding where the good local motorcycle roads were. Making matters worse was that as my life got busier with family and career responsibilities, I found that I had less time to go out hunting for good roads. So back in 2014 I decided to start urbanriderz.in as a way for all of us to quickly and easily find quality motorcycle roads in whatever part of the country we live in. And urbanriderz.in goes beyond just providing you with a list of roads to try, we provide you with detailed descriptions of the routes scenery, road quality, and even the things to do along the way (roadside amenities). The route descriptions often times includes pictures, reviews and ratings from other riders, and even videos of the route and now the site even tells you of motorcycle events, clubs, and hangouts along the route and within the state. Overall, hundreds of motorcycle riders are discovering that visiting urbanriderz.in is the first step in finding and planning great motorcycle rides.

since every riders route describes and rates these three key qualities, you will be able to find roads that match what you are looking for. Not only do we describe the route but we give you vital information including directions to and through the route (written directions and a map), we provide you with demographics of the contributor such as name or nickname, when the route was contributed, and the contributor’s motorcycle type. We also let you know the length of the route, tips about what there is to see or stop at along the way, and even give you a link to the route’s real-time weather forecast (weather forecast for the start or end point).
